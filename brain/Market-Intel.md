**Filed:** 2026-05-14 (Ogie msg, post-Hyperbridge triage)

### The signal

- **Code4rena and Sherlock are shutting down competitive audit contests.**
- 2026 has had only **4 major Solidity contests so far**.
- **2 of 4 closed with ZERO findings.**

### Source quote

> "With C4 and Sherlock both shutting down contests, verifiable public proof of work is gone entirely — making an active track record from the final contests the most credible signal left in the industry."
> — cool_fool, Pashov Audit Group Discord, 2026-05-13

### Strategic implications

1. **The audit contest discovery model is DYING.** The competitive-public-leaderboard era is closing within the next ~6-12 months.
2. **Bug bounty programs become the primary discovery vector** for both pre-deployment and post-deployment security. The supply of "researcher hours" that used to flow into contests now needs to land somewhere.
3. **AI-assisted autonomous hunting fills the supply gap** left by displaced contest researchers. The market for finished bugs is unchanged; the labor pool feeding it is shrinking.
4. **Methodology-as-product becomes the moat** — no public leaderboards = no fast pattern-matching on top performers. Reputation transfers via track record + buyable methodology, not via Code4rena standings.
5. **Pashov inversion thesis STRENGTHENED:** old model = hire the displaced researchers; new model = build the autonomous methodology that replaces them.
