## CROSS-POLLINATION ENGINE

The part nobody else can do at scale.

Pattern: finding in protocol A → productized detector → automatically checked
against protocols B through Z on every future scan. Brain remembers. Detector
encodes. Every scan inherits.

### Live example (Sunday May 10-11):

H4 dYdX → detector #165 → catches C-1 + C-3 Composable + S-1 Stride
One finding → one detector → three more findings across three protocols
Discipline became infrastructure.

### Compound math:

detectors × protocols_scanned_weekly = cross_checks_weekly
6 × 10 = 60
10 × 30 = 300
20 × 50 = 1,000
30 × 100 = 3,000

At 30 detectors × 100 protocols/week = 3,000 cross-checks/week.
No human team matches this surface coverage.

### Standing directive:

Every accepted finding MUST spawn a productization assessment:

- Can this pattern class be encoded as a detector?
- If YES → file detector spec, queue for build, assign P-level
- If NO → file as ground truth + worked example (brain still learns)
- NEVER let a finding die as a one-off report

## DECISION FRAMEWORK (read on every decision)

For every task, scan, outreach, or content decision, ask:

1. **Does this compound the brain?**
   (doctrine, worked example, detector, ground truth, clean-scan data)

2. **Does this feed a revenue lane?**
   (hunting bounty, HSaaS prospect, visibility/content)

3. **Does this maintain discipline?**
   (standing rules, 5-gate, War Room approval, no shortcuts)

```
YES to 1 + 2 + 3 → EXECUTE (optimal)
YES to 1 + 3 but not 2 → EXECUTE (infrastructure investment)
YES to 2 but not 1 or 3 → HOLD (revenue without methodology = noise)
NO to all → DEFER (distraction)
```

Every cycle is either building the brain, feeding a lane, or maintaining
discipline. Anything else is a distraction. Defer it.
